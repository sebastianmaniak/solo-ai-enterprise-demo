# Certificate of Deposit (CD) Rates

**Effective Date:** April 1, 2026
**Next Review:** May 1, 2026

## Overview

Solo Bank offers Certificates of Deposit with fixed interest rates for terms ranging from 3 months to 5 years. CD rates are locked at the time of opening and do not change for the duration of the term. All CDs are FDIC insured up to $250,000 per depositor, per ownership category.

## Early Withdrawal Penalties

Withdrawing funds from a CD before the maturity date incurs an early withdrawal penalty. The penalty is deducted from the interest earned; if the interest earned is insufficient to cover the penalty, the difference is deducted from the principal.

| CD Term | Early Withdrawal Penalty |
|---|---|
| Less than 12 months | 90 days of interest |
| 12–35 months | 180 days of interest |
| 36–59 months | 270 days of interest |
| 60 months | 365 days of interest |

### Early Withdrawal Penalty Examples

**Example 1: 12-month CD, $10,000 at 4.50% APY, withdrawn after 6 months**
- Interest earned in 6 months: approximately $225
- Penalty: 180 days of interest = approximately $222
- Net interest received: approximately $3
- Principal returned in full: $10,000

**Example 2: 6-month CD, $5,000 at 4.25% APY, withdrawn after 2 months**
- Interest earned in 2 months: approximately $35
- Penalty: 90 days of interest = approximately $53
- Shortfall deducted from principal: $18
- Amount received: $4,982 + $35 interest - $53 penalty = $4,982

**Example 3: 36-month CD, $25,000 at 4.10% APY, withdrawn after 12 months**
- Interest earned in 12 months: approximately $1,025
- Penalty: 270 days of interest = approximately $759
- Net interest received: approximately $266
- Principal returned in full: $25,000

## Interest Payment Options

- **Compound to CD:** Interest is compounded daily and credited to the CD monthly, increasing the effective yield. This is the default option.
- **Transfer to Solo Bank Account:** Monthly interest is transferred to a designated Solo Bank checking or savings account.
- **Mail Check:** Monthly interest is mailed to the customer's address on file (available for CDs of $10,000 or more).

## Maturity and Renewal

- Solo Bank sends a maturity notice 30 days before the CD maturity date via mail and electronic notification.
- **Grace Period:** There is a **10-calendar-day grace period** after the maturity date during which the customer may:
  - Withdraw the funds without penalty
  - Renew the CD at the then-current rate for the same or different term
  - Add funds to the CD (subject to the same-term renewal)
- **Automatic Renewal:** If the customer takes no action during the grace period, the CD automatically renews for the same term at the current rate offered on the renewal date. The entire balance (principal plus accrued interest) is rolled into the new CD.

## Special CD Products

### No-Penalty CD (11-Month)

- **APY:** 4.10%
- **Minimum Deposit:** $5,000
- **Penalty:** None — full withdrawal allowed at any time after the first 7 days
- **Partial Withdrawal:** Not permitted — must withdraw the full balance
- **Limit:** One No-Penalty CD per customer

### Bump-Up CD (24-Month)

- **APY:** 4.00% (initial)
- **Minimum Deposit:** $5,000
- **Bump-Up Option:** Customer may request a one-time rate increase to the then-current 24-month CD rate at any point during the term
- **Early Withdrawal Penalty:** 180 days of interest (standard 24-month penalty applies)

## Eligibility

- CDs are available to all Solo Bank customers in good standing.
- No minimum credit score required.
- Business accounts and trust accounts are eligible.
- IRA CDs are available with the same rates and terms (subject to IRA contribution and distribution rules).

## Important Disclosures

- APYs are accurate as of the effective date shown and assume interest remains on deposit until maturity.
- A penalty for early withdrawal may reduce earnings below the stated APY or result in a loss of principal.
- Fees on the account could reduce earnings.
- Rates and terms are set by the Solo Bank Asset-Liability Committee (ALCO) and may change at any time for new CDs. Existing CD rates are not affected by rate changes.
