# Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

**Product Code:** LN-HELOC-008
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026

## Product Summary

The Solo Bank Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) allows homeowners to borrow against the equity in their primary residence. The HELOC features a variable interest rate tied to the U.S. Prime Rate, a 10-year draw period during which the borrower can access funds as needed, and a 20-year repayment period. This product is commonly used for home improvements, debt consolidation, education expenses, and other major expenditures.

## Eligibility

- **Credit Tier Required:** Tier 1 (800–850), Tier 2 (740–799), Tier 3 (670–739), or Tier 4 (580–669)
- **Tier 5 (300–579):** Not eligible for HELOC
- **Property Type:** Owner-occupied primary residence only (no second homes or investment properties)
- **Minimum Equity:** At least 20% equity in the home after accounting for the HELOC
- **Maximum Combined Loan-to-Value (CLTV):** 80%
- **Minimum Credit Score:** 580 (Tier 4)
- **Maximum Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI):** 43%
- **Minimum Line Amount:** $10,000
- **Maximum Line Amount:** $500,000

## Interest Rates

The HELOC rate is variable, based on the U.S. Prime Rate plus a margin that depends on the borrower's credit tier:

| Credit Tier | Score Range | Margin | Current Rate (Prime 8.50%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Excellent | 800–850 | Prime + 1.00% | 9.50% |
| Tier 2 — Very Good | 740–799 | Prime + 1.50% | 10.00% |
| Tier 3 — Good | 670–739 | Prime + 2.50% | 11.00% |
| Tier 4 — Fair | 580–669 | Prime + 3.50% | 12.00% |

### Rate Adjustments

- The rate adjusts on the **first business day of each month** following a change in the Prime Rate.
- **Rate Floor:** The interest rate will never fall below 4.00% APR, regardless of the Prime Rate.
- **Rate Ceiling:** The interest rate will never exceed 18.00% APR.
- There is no introductory or promotional rate on the HELOC.

### Relationship Discounts

Existing Solo Bank customers may qualify for additional rate reductions per the [Credit Score Tiers](../policies/credit-score-tiers.md) relationship discount schedule:
- 5–9 years: -0.125%
- 10+ years: -0.250%

## CLTV Calculation

The Combined Loan-to-Value ratio determines the maximum HELOC amount:

```
CLTV = (Outstanding Mortgage Balance + HELOC Line Amount) / Appraised Home Value
```

The CLTV must not exceed **80%**.

### Example

- Appraised home value: $500,000
- Outstanding mortgage balance: $300,000
- Maximum CLTV: 80% x $500,000 = $400,000
- Maximum HELOC line: $400,000 - $300,000 = **$100,000**

## Draw Period (Years 1–10)

During the 10-year draw period:

- The borrower may draw funds as needed, up to the approved credit line amount.
- Funds can be accessed via:
  - Online banking transfer to a Solo Bank checking or savings account
  - HELOC checks (provided at no charge)
  - Phone request (call customer service)
  - Branch visit
- **Minimum Draw:** $500 per transaction
- **Interest-Only Payments:** During the draw period, the minimum monthly payment is **interest only** on the outstanding balance.
- The borrower may make principal payments at any time during the draw period without penalty.
- The credit line revolves — as the borrower repays principal, the available credit is restored and can be borrowed again.

## Repayment Period (Years 11–30)

At the end of the 10-year draw period, the HELOC enters a 20-year repayment period:

- No additional draws are permitted during the repayment period.
- Monthly payments convert to **fully amortizing principal and interest** payments based on the outstanding balance and remaining 20-year term.
- The interest rate continues to be variable (Prime + margin), adjusting monthly.
- There is no prepayment penalty — the borrower may pay off the balance at any time.

### Repayment Example

A borrower with a $75,000 outstanding balance at the start of the repayment period with a rate of 9.50%:
- Estimated monthly payment: approximately **$700** (principal and interest)
- This payment will fluctuate as the variable rate adjusts.

3. Most recent 2 years of W-2 forms or tax returns

5. Most recent mortgage statement
6. Homeowners insurance declaration page
7. Most recent property tax bill

## Approval Process

1. **Application:** Submit online, by phone, or at a branch. A credit pull is performed.
2. **Appraisal:** Solo Bank orders an appraisal from an approved appraiser. The borrower pays the appraisal fee.
3. **Title Search:** Title company verifies clear title and determines lien position.
4. **Underwriting:** The underwriter reviews the application, income documentation, appraisal, and credit history. Target: 5 business days.
5. **Approval/Conditions:** The borrower is notified of approval with any conditions to be satisfied.
6. **Closing:** Documents are signed (may be done at a branch, with a notary, or via mobile notary). The HELOC is recorded as a second lien on the property.
7. **3-Day Right of Rescission:** Per federal regulation (TILA), the borrower has 3 business days after closing to cancel the HELOC without penalty.
8. **Funding:** After the rescission period, the credit line is available for draws.

## Tax Considerations

- Interest paid on a HELOC may be tax-deductible if the funds are used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home that secures the loan.
- Under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA), HELOC interest used for other purposes (debt consolidation, education, etc.) is generally not deductible.
- Solo Bank provides a Form 1098 annually showing interest paid.
- Borrowers should consult a tax advisor for guidance specific to their situation.

## Important Disclosures

- The HELOC is secured by the borrower's primary residence. Failure to make payments may result in foreclosure.
- Solo Bank may reduce the credit limit or freeze the line if the home value declines significantly, if the borrower's financial condition deteriorates, or if the borrower fails to maintain homeowners insurance.
- The variable rate means monthly payments can increase if the Prime Rate rises.
