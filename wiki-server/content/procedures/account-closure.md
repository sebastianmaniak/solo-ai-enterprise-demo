# Account Closure Procedure

**Procedure ID:** PROC-AC-005
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** February 15, 2026

## Overview

This procedure outlines the steps for closing a consumer deposit account at Solo Bank, whether initiated by the customer or by the bank. Proper account closure ensures all obligations are settled, linked services are cancelled, regulatory requirements are met, and the customer's records are retained per policy.

## Account Closure Channels

Customers may request account closure through the following channels:

- **In-Branch:** Visit any Solo Bank branch with a government-issued photo ID.
- **Phone:** Call 1-800-SOLO-BANK and speak with a customer service representative.
- **Written Request:** Mail a signed closure request to Solo Bank, P.O. Box 12345, Wilmington, DE 19801.

**Note:** Account closure cannot be completed through online banking or the mobile app. A verbal or written request with identity verification is required.

## Step 1: Verify Customer Identity

Before proceeding with any account closure:

1. **In-Branch:** Verify the customer's identity with a government-issued photo ID. Compare the ID photo and name to the customer record in the Core Banking System.
2. **Phone:** Verify the customer's identity using account number, last 4 digits of SSN, date of birth, and security question. For accounts with balances over $5,000, a callback to the phone number on file is required.
3. **Written Request:** The letter must include the customer's full name, account number, date of birth, last 4 of SSN, and a notarized signature. If the signature is not notarized, contact the customer to verify the request before proceeding.

### Joint Accounts

- Either account holder on a joint account may request closure.
- The closing balance will be distributed as directed by the requesting account holder unless the other account holder has placed a restriction on the account.
- Both account holders are notified of the closure via mail within 5 business days.

## Step 2: Check for Holds and Pending Transactions

Before closing the account, verify the following:

### Pending Transactions

- Check for any **pending debit card authorizations** that have not yet posted.
- Check for any **pending ACH debits or credits** (incoming payroll, scheduled bill payments, automatic transfers).
- Check for any **pending mobile check deposits** that have not yet cleared.
- If pending transactions exist, inform the customer and either:
  - Wait for all pending transactions to post before closing (recommended), OR
  - Close the account after the customer acknowledges that pending transactions may be returned unpaid.

### Holds

- Check for any **check deposit holds** (Regulation CC holds on recently deposited checks).
- Check for any **legal holds** (garnishments, levies, subpoenas). If a legal hold exists, **do not close the account** until the hold is released or legal counsel approves the closure.
- Check for any **fraud investigation holds**. If the account is under active fraud investigation, the closure must be approved by the Fraud Investigations team.

### Open Disputes

- If there are any open transaction disputes on the account, inform the customer that the dispute will continue to be processed after closure. The resolution (credit or reversal) will be applied to the closed account and disbursed by check.

## Step 3: Transfer or Disburse Remaining Balance

The remaining balance must be disbursed to the customer. Available options:

| Method | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cash (in-branch) | Immediate | For balances up to $5,000. Larger amounts require advance notice (24 hours) and Branch Manager approval. |
| Cashier's check | Immediate (in-branch) | Issued to the account holder. No fee for account closure disbursements. |
| ACH transfer to external bank | 1–3 business days | Customer provides routing and account number. |
| Wire transfer | Same day | $25 domestic / $45 international fee applies. |
| Check mailed | 7–10 business days | Mailed to the address on file. |

### Negative Balance

- If the account has a **negative balance** (overdrawn), the customer must bring the account to a $0 balance before closure.
- If the customer is unable to pay the negative balance, the account is closed as a **charge-off** after 60 days, and the amount is reported to ChexSystems and may be referred to collections.
- Charge-off closures require Branch Manager approval and documentation on Form CO-200.

### Interest and Accrued Earnings

- For interest-bearing accounts (savings, money market, CDs), any accrued but unpaid interest through the date of closure is calculated and included in the final disbursement.
- For CDs closed before maturity, the **early withdrawal penalty** is deducted from the disbursement per [CD Rates](../rates/cd-rates.md).

## Step 4: Cancel Linked Services

The following linked services must be reviewed and cancelled or transferred:

1. **Debit Card:** Deactivate the debit card immediately. The customer should destroy the physical card.
2. **Online Banking:** The online banking profile is deactivated at the time of closure. Historical statements remain accessible for 90 days after closure.
3. **Overdraft Protection Links:** If the account being closed is linked as the funding source for another account's Overdraft Protection, remove the link and notify the customer.
4. **Overdraft Line of Credit:** If an Overdraft Line of Credit is attached, it must be paid off and closed separately.
5. **Automatic Payments (ACH Debits):** Advise the customer to update any recurring payments that debit the account (utilities, subscriptions, loan payments, insurance). Provide a list of recent ACH debits from the last 3 months to assist.
6. **Direct Deposit:** Advise the customer to update their direct deposit information with their employer before the account is closed. Provide the customer's new bank routing/account information form if they are moving to another institution.
7. **Bill Pay Payees:** Cancel all scheduled bill pay payments. Inform the customer of any payments scheduled within the next 30 days that will not be processed.
8. **Safe Deposit Box:** If the customer has a safe deposit box, it must be emptied and the rental agreement terminated separately (at a branch).
9. **HELOC or Loan Linked to Account:** If a HELOC or loan has automatic payments drawn from the account, the customer must set up an alternate payment method before closure.

## Step 5: Early Closure Fee Assessment

- If the account has been open for **fewer than 90 days**, an **early closure fee of $25.00** applies.
- The fee is deducted from the remaining balance before disbursement.
- The early closure fee does not apply if the bank initiated the closure or if the account was opened in error.

## Step 6: Close the Account in the Core Banking System

1. Enter the closure reason code in the CBS:
   - **Customer Requested** — customer-initiated voluntary closure
   - **Bank Initiated — Inactivity** — dormant account closed by the bank after 24 months of no activity
   - **Bank Initiated — Negative Balance** — charge-off closure
   - **Bank Initiated — Fraud** — closed due to confirmed fraud
   - **Bank Initiated — Compliance** — closed due to KYC/AML concerns
2. Record the disbursement method and amount.
3. Mark the account status as **CLOSED** in the CBS.
4. The CBS generates a closure confirmation for the customer record.

## Step 7: Confirmation Letter

- A **written confirmation letter** is mailed to the customer's address on file within **5 business days** of closure.
- The letter includes:
  - Account number (masked)
  - Date of closure
  - Final balance and disbursement method
  - Summary of any fees charged at closure
  - Contact information for questions
  - Notice that the final 1099-INT (if applicable) will be mailed in January of the following year

## Step 8: Record Retention

- All account records, including the closure documentation, statements, transaction history, KYC documents, and signed agreements, are retained for **7 years** from the date of closure.
- Records are stored electronically in the Core Banking System archive.
- Records are accessible for regulatory examinations, legal proceedings, and customer inquiries during the retention period.

## Bank-Initiated Closures

Solo Bank may close an account without customer request under the following circumstances:

- **Dormancy:** No customer-initiated activity for 24 months. Before closure, a dormancy notice is mailed 60 days in advance. Unclaimed funds are escheated to the state per applicable unclaimed property laws.
- **Persistent Overdraft:** Account overdrawn for 60 or more consecutive days with no arrangement to repay.
- **Fraud:** Confirmed fraudulent activity on or through the account.
- **Compliance:** Account poses unacceptable BSA/AML risk as determined by the BSA Officer.
- **Terms Violation:** Customer has violated the account agreement terms.

For all bank-initiated closures, the customer is notified in writing at least **30 days before** closure (except for fraud and compliance closures, which may be immediate with concurrent notice).
