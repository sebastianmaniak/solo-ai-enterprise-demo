# Loan Application Process

**Procedure ID:** PROC-LA-003
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026

## Overview

This procedure outlines the end-to-end process for residential mortgage loan applications at Solo Bank, from initial pre-qualification through closing. It applies to all mortgage products: 30-Year Fixed, 15-Year Fixed, 5/1 ARM, and Jumbo 30-Year Fixed. For HELOC applications, see the HELOC-specific sections in [Home Equity Line of Credit](../products/home-equity-loc.md).

## Process Summary

| Step | Description | Target Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pre-Qualification | Same day |
| 2 | Application | 1–2 days |
| 3 | Processing | 5 business days |
| 4 | Underwriting | 3 business days |
| 5 | Conditional Approval | 1 business day |
| 6 | Clear to Close | 2–5 business days |
| 7 | Closing | Scheduled date |

**Total target timeline:** 30–45 calendar days from application to closing.

## Step 1: Pre-Qualification

### Purpose

Pre-qualification gives the borrower an estimate of how much they may be able to borrow, based on self-reported financial information. It is not a commitment to lend.

### Process

1. The borrower provides self-reported information: annual income, monthly debts, estimated credit score, desired loan amount, and down payment.
2. The Loan Officer reviews the information and estimates the borrower's purchasing power using Solo Bank's lending guidelines.
3. **No credit pull** is performed at this stage.
4. The Loan Officer provides a pre-qualification letter (valid for 90 days) if the borrower appears to meet basic eligibility requirements per [Mortgage Lending Policy](../policies/mortgage-lending.md).

### Pre-Qualification Does NOT Guarantee

- A specific interest rate
- Loan approval
- A specific loan amount
- Product eligibility

## Step 2: Application

### Process

1. The borrower completes the **Uniform Residential Loan Application (URLA / Form 1003)**. This can be done online at solobank.com/mortgage, at a branch, or by phone with a Loan Officer.

2. The borrower provides authorization for Solo Bank to pull credit from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion).

3. The Loan Officer pulls credit and determines the borrower's **credit tier** per [Credit Score Tiers](../policies/credit-score-tiers.md). The middle score of the three bureaus is used.

4. The Loan Officer reviews the application for completeness and identifies the loan products for which the borrower may be eligible.

5. The borrower is provided with:
   - **Loan Estimate (LE)** within **3 business days** of application (required by TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure Rule)
   - List of required documentation (see below)
   - Rate sheet showing available rates and lock options

### Required Documentation

The borrower must submit the following:

1. Government-issued photo ID (copy)
2. Social Security Number (provided on application)
3. Most recent **2 years of W-2 forms** (or tax returns for self-employed)
4. Most recent **2 months of pay stubs**
5. Most recent **3 months of bank statements** (all accounts showing assets for down payment and reserves)
6. Signed **employment verification authorization**
7. **Purchase agreement** (for purchases) or **current mortgage statement** (for refinances)
8. **Proof of homeowners insurance** (binder or declaration page)
9. **Gift letter** (if any portion of the down payment is a gift, including documentation of the gift source)

## Step 3: Processing

### Process

The Loan Processor prepares the file for underwriting by ordering and verifying all third-party information:

1. **Order Appraisal:** Engage a Solo Bank-approved appraiser. Appraisal fee ($450–$700) is collected from the borrower. Target turnaround: 7–10 business days.
2. **Title Search:** Order a title search and title insurance commitment from an approved title company.
3. **Employment Verification:** Contact the borrower's employer (via phone or written verification) to confirm employment status, income, and tenure.
4. **Verification of Deposits (VOD):** Confirm bank account balances and transaction history for the accounts listed on the application.
5. **Flood Determination:** Determine whether the property is in a FEMA-designated flood zone. If yes, flood insurance is required.
6. **Verification of Rent or Mortgage:** Confirm the borrower's current housing payment history (12 months).
7. **Review Documentation:** Ensure all required documents are complete, legible, and consistent with the application data.

### Processing Checklist

The processor marks each item as received and verified in the Loan Origination System (LOS). The file is not submitted to underwriting until all items are complete or a clear explanation is documented for missing items.

## Step 4: Underwriting

### Process

The Underwriter reviews the complete loan file and evaluates the borrower's creditworthiness and the property's suitability as collateral:

1. **Credit Analysis:** Review credit report, payment history, outstanding debts, and credit tier assignment.
2. **Income Analysis:** Verify that documented income supports the loan payment. Calculate DTI ratio (must not exceed 43%).
3. **Asset Analysis:** Confirm the borrower has sufficient funds for the down payment, closing costs, and reserves.
4. **Appraisal Review:** Confirm the appraised value supports the loan amount and the property meets condition requirements.
5. **Title Review:** Confirm clear title with no liens, judgments, or encumbrances that would affect the mortgage position.
6. **Compliance Review:** Ensure the loan meets all Solo Bank policies and applicable regulations.

### Underwriting Authority

| Borrower Tier | Underwriting Method |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 (800–850) | Automated underwriting with manual review override |
| Tier 2 (740–799) | Automated underwriting with manual review override |
| Tier 3 (670–739) | Full manual underwriting required |
| Tier 4 (580–669) | Full manual underwriting + Branch Manager sign-off |

### Underwriting Decisions

The underwriter will issue one of the following:

- **Approved:** The loan is fully approved with no outstanding conditions.
- **Conditionally Approved:** The loan is approved subject to specific conditions being met (most common outcome).
- **Suspended:** The file is incomplete; additional information is required before a decision can be made. The file is returned to the processor.
- **Denied:** The loan does not meet policy requirements. The borrower receives a written adverse action notice within 30 days per ECOA/Regulation B.

## Step 5: Conditional Approval

### Common Conditions

Conditions typically fall into two categories:

**Prior to Documents (PTD):** Must be satisfied before closing documents can be prepared.
- Updated pay stub within 30 days of closing
- Letter of explanation for credit inquiries or large deposits
- Verification of source of gift funds
- Updated bank statement

**Prior to Funding (PTF):** Must be satisfied before the loan can be funded.
- Proof of homeowners insurance with Solo Bank listed as mortgagee
- Final title insurance policy
- Clear termite inspection (if required)
- Signed closing documents

### Condition Clearance

The processor collects the required items, and the underwriter reviews and clears each condition. Once all PTD conditions are cleared, the file moves to Clear to Close.

## Step 6: Clear to Close

1. The underwriter issues a **Clear to Close** (CTC) status.
2. The Closing Department prepares the **Closing Disclosure (CD)**, which details the final loan terms, closing costs, and cash required at closing.
3. The Closing Disclosure must be delivered to the borrower at least **3 business days** before the scheduled closing date (TRID rule).
4. The borrower reviews the CD and confirms the terms. Any changes to the CD that result in an increase in APR of more than 0.125% or a change in loan product require a new 3-day waiting period.
5. The closing is scheduled at a title company, attorney's office, or Solo Bank branch.

## Step 7: Closing

### Closing Day Process

1. The borrower and any co-borrowers sign all closing documents, including the promissory note, mortgage/deed of trust, and closing disclosure.
2. The borrower provides a certified or cashier's check (or wire transfer) for the cash-to-close amount.
3. The title company disburses funds to the seller (purchase) or pays off the existing lender (refinance).
4. The mortgage is recorded with the county recorder's office.
5. The borrower receives copies of all signed documents and the keys to the property (for purchases).

### Post-Closing

- The loan is boarded into Solo Bank's servicing system within 3 business days.
- A welcome package is mailed to the borrower with payment instructions, online access setup, and escrow account details.
- The first payment is typically due 30–60 days after closing.
