# Dispute Resolution Procedure

**Procedure ID:** PROC-DR-002
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026

## Overview

This procedure governs the process for handling customer disputes related to unauthorized transactions, billing errors, and merchant disputes on Solo Bank debit cards, credit cards, and deposit accounts. The process must comply with Regulation E (Electronic Fund Transfers Act) for debit card and electronic transactions and Regulation Z (Truth in Lending Act) for credit card transactions.

## Scope

This procedure applies to disputes involving:

- Unauthorized debit card transactions
- Unauthorized credit card transactions
- ATM transaction errors
- ACH/electronic transfer errors
- Billing errors (duplicate charges, incorrect amounts)
- Goods or services not received or not as described
- Recurring charges after cancellation

## Step 1: Dispute Initiation

### How Customers Can File a Dispute

Customers may initiate a dispute through any of the following channels:

- **Phone:** Call 1-800-SOLO-BANK and select the disputes option
- **Online Banking:** Navigate to the transaction and select "Dispute This Transaction"
- **Mobile App:** Tap the transaction and select "Report a Problem"
- **In-Branch:** Complete a Dispute Form (Form DSP-100) with a banker
- **Mail:** Send a written dispute to Solo Bank, P.O. Box 12345, Wilmington, DE 19801

### Information Required

The customer must provide:

1. Full name and account number
2. Transaction date(s) and amount(s) in dispute
3. Merchant name (if applicable)
4. Reason for the dispute (unauthorized, incorrect amount, goods not received, etc.)
5. Whether the card was lost or stolen (if applicable)
6. Date the customer discovered the error
7. Any supporting documentation (receipts, correspondence with merchant, cancellation confirmations)

### Time Limits for Filing

- **Debit card / electronic transfers (Reg E):** Customer must report the error within **60 days** of the statement date on which the error first appeared.
- **Credit card (Reg Z):** Customer must report the error within **60 days** of the statement date on which the error first appeared.
- Disputes filed after the 60-day window may still be investigated but are not subject to the provisional credit requirements.

## Step 2: Case Assignment and Acknowledgment

- Upon receipt, the dispute is entered into the Dispute Management System (DMS) and assigned a unique case number.
- The customer receives an acknowledgment within **1 business day** via email (or mail if no email on file) confirming:
  - Case number
  - Summary of the disputed transaction(s)
  - Expected timeline for resolution
  - Contact information for the disputes team
- The dispute is assigned to a Dispute Analyst based on complexity and case load.

## Step 3: Provisional Credit

### Debit Card / Electronic Transfers (Regulation E)

- Solo Bank must provide **provisional credit** within **10 business days** of receiving the dispute if the investigation is not yet complete.
- The provisional credit equals the full amount of the disputed transaction(s).
- The customer is notified in writing when provisional credit is issued.
- If the investigation determines the transaction was authorized or the error did not occur, the provisional credit will be reversed with **5 business days advance written notice** to the customer.

### Credit Card (Regulation Z)

- For credit card disputes classified as billing errors, the disputed amount is **not due** during the investigation period. The bank may not report the disputed amount as delinquent.
- No provisional credit is required under Reg Z, but Solo Bank's practice is to issue a **temporary credit** within **10 business days** for disputed amounts over $50 as a customer service measure.
- Minimum payment calculations exclude the disputed amount during the investigation.

### Authority for Provisional Credits

Per the [Customer Service Escalation Policy](../policies/customer-service-escalation.md):

- **L1 representatives** may issue provisional credits up to **$500**
- **L2 supervisors** may issue provisional credits up to **$5,000**
- **L3 senior management** may issue provisional credits of **any amount**

## Step 4: Investigation

### Investigation Timeline

| Transaction Type | Maximum Investigation Period |
|---|---|
| Debit card / electronic (Reg E) — standard | 45 calendar days |
| Debit card / electronic (Reg E) — new account (<30 days), POS, or international | 90 calendar days |
| Credit card (Reg Z) | 60 calendar days (2 complete billing cycles, not to exceed 90 days) |

### Investigation Steps

1. **Transaction Review:** Analyst reviews the transaction details, authorization records, and merchant information in the payment network system.

2. **Customer Interview:** If additional information is needed, the analyst contacts the customer by phone or secure message. If the customer cannot be reached within 2 business days, a letter is mailed.

3. **Merchant Outreach:** For billing disputes and goods/services disputes, the analyst may contact the merchant through the payment network's chargeback process.

4. **Documentation Review:** Analyze any supporting documents provided by the customer or merchant (receipts, delivery confirmations, contracts, cancellation records).

5. **Fraud Analysis:** For unauthorized transaction claims, the Fraud Detection team reviews the transaction for indicators of fraud or account takeover. See [Fraud Detection Policy](../policies/fraud-detection.md).

6. **Chargeback Filing:** If the dispute involves a card transaction and the investigation supports the customer's claim, a chargeback is filed with the payment network (Visa/Mastercard) within network timelines.

7. **Decision:** The analyst makes a determination based on the evidence:
   - **Customer's claim upheld:** Provisional credit becomes permanent; case closed.
   - **Customer's claim denied:** Provisional credit reversed (with 5 business days notice); customer notified of reason and right to request documentation.

## Step 5: Resolution and Notification

### Resolution Letter

Upon completion of the investigation, Solo Bank sends a written resolution letter to the customer within **3 business days** of the decision. The letter includes:

- Case number and date of original dispute
- Summary of the investigation findings
- Final decision (upheld or denied)
- If denied: specific reason(s), the customer's right to request copies of documents relied upon, and instructions for escalation
- If upheld: confirmation that the credit is permanent

### If the Customer Disagrees

- The customer may request to **escalate** the decision per the [Customer Service Escalation Policy](../policies/customer-service-escalation.md).
- The customer may provide additional evidence within 10 business days of receiving the resolution letter for a **re-review**.
- If the customer remains dissatisfied, they may file a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) or the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC).

## Special Scenarios

### Lost or Stolen Card

- If the customer reports a card as lost or stolen, the card is **immediately blocked** and a replacement card is issued.
- All transactions occurring after the reported date of loss/theft are automatically disputed.
- The customer's liability for unauthorized transactions:
  - Reported within 2 business days: max **$50** liability
  - Reported after 2 business days but within 60 days: max **$500** liability
  - Reported after 60 days: potentially unlimited liability for subsequent unauthorized transactions

### Recurring Charges

- If the customer disputes a recurring charge they claim to have canceled, the analyst will request proof of cancellation.
- Solo Bank will block future charges from the merchant if the customer requests (merchant block added to the card).

### ATM Errors

- ATM disputes (wrong amount dispensed, deposit not credited) are investigated by comparing the ATM journal tape, security camera footage, and settlement records.
- Resolution target: 10 business days.

## Record Retention

- All dispute documentation, investigation notes, correspondence, and resolution letters are retained for **7 years** from the date of case closure.
- Records are stored in the Dispute Management System and are accessible for audit and regulatory examination.
