# New Account Opening Procedure

**Procedure ID:** PROC-NAO-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** February 15, 2026

## Overview

This procedure outlines the step-by-step process for opening a new consumer deposit account at Solo Bank. It applies to all account types: Basic Checking, Premium Checking, Basic Savings, High-Yield Savings, Money Market, and Certificates of Deposit. All staff involved in account opening must follow these steps in order to ensure regulatory compliance, accurate account setup, and a positive customer experience.

## Pre-Requisites

Before beginning the account opening process, ensure the following:

- You have access to the Core Banking System (CBS) and the KYC/AML screening tools.
- The customer is physically present (for in-branch opening) or has initiated the online application (for digital opening).
- You have reviewed the current [Account Types](../policies/account-types.md) and [Fee Schedule](../policies/fee-schedule.md) to answer any product questions.

## Step 1: Verify Customer Identity (KYC)

### Required Documents

Collect and verify the following from the customer:

1. **Government-Issued Photo ID** — Valid, unexpired U.S. driver's license, state ID, passport, or passport card. For non-U.S. persons: valid foreign passport with visa, or Permanent Resident Card.
2. **Social Security Number (SSN)** or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).
3. **Proof of Address** — One of the following dated within the past 60 days: utility bill, bank statement, lease agreement, mortgage statement, or voter registration card.

### Verification Process

- Enter the customer's information into the KYC verification system.
- The system will electronically verify the SSN against the Social Security Administration database.
- Compare the photo on the ID with the customer present.
- If electronic SSN verification fails, require two additional forms of identification and escalate to the Branch Manager for review.

**Regulatory Basis:** USA PATRIOT Act Section 326, FinCEN Customer Identification Program (CIP) Rule. See [KYC/AML Compliance Policy](../policies/kyc-aml-compliance.md).

## Step 2: OFAC Screening

- The Core Banking System automatically screens the customer's name, date of birth, and address against the **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List**.
- If a potential match is found, **STOP** — do not proceed with account opening. Notify the BSA/AML Officer immediately. Do not inform the customer of the reason for the hold.
- If no match is found, the system records a clear OFAC check and the process continues.

## Step 3: ChexSystems Review

- The system automatically pulls a **ChexSystems report** to check for prior negative banking history (unpaid fees, involuntary account closures, fraud flags).
- **Clear report:** Proceed with account opening.
- **Negative report (non-fraud):** The customer may be offered a Basic Checking account or Basic Savings account at the Branch Manager's discretion. Document the decision.
- **Fraud flag:** Do not open the account. Refer to the BSA/AML Officer.

## Step 4: Product Selection and Disclosure

### Product Consultation

- Review the customer's needs and recommend an appropriate product.
- Explain the key features, fees, and requirements of the selected account:
  - Monthly maintenance fees and waiver conditions
  - Minimum balance requirements
  - Interest rates (if applicable)
  - Overdraft options
  - ATM access and fees

### Required Disclosures

Provide the customer with the following documents (paper or electronic):

1. **Account Agreement and Disclosures** — Terms and conditions for the account type
2. **Truth in Savings Disclosure (Regulation DD)** — APY, interest calculation method, fees, and balance requirements
3. **Electronic Fund Transfer Disclosure (Regulation E)** — Rights and responsibilities for electronic transactions
4. **Privacy Notice** — How Solo Bank collects, uses, and shares personal information
5. **Overdraft Opt-In Form** — For checking accounts, present the Regulation E opt-in form for ATM/debit card overdraft coverage (see [Overdraft Policy](../policies/overdraft-policy.md))
6. **Funds Availability Disclosure (Regulation CC)** — When deposited funds will be available for withdrawal

The customer must acknowledge receipt of all disclosures. For in-branch opening, the customer signs the acknowledgment form. For online opening, the customer checks the acknowledgment box.

## Step 5: Account Funding

The account must be funded at the time of opening or within 30 calendar days. Accepted funding methods:

| Method | Availability |
|---|---|
| Cash (in-branch) | Immediate |
| Personal check | Next business day (up to $225 available immediately) |
| Cashier's check / certified check | Next business day (full amount) |
| Wire transfer | Same day (if received before 4 PM ET) |
| ACH transfer from external bank | 1–3 business days |
| Internal transfer from existing Solo Bank account | Immediate |

### Minimum Opening Deposits

| Account Type | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Basic Checking | $25 |
| Premium Checking | $5,000 |
| Basic Savings | $0 |
| High-Yield Savings | $1,000 |
| Money Market | $10,000 |
| Certificate of Deposit | $1,000 |

## Step 6: Debit Card Issuance (Checking Accounts)

- **In-Branch:** Issue a temporary debit card on the spot (fee: $5 for Basic Checking; free for Premium Checking). Order the permanent card for mail delivery (7–10 business days).
- **Online Opening:** The permanent debit card is mailed to the address on file within 7–10 business days. Rush delivery (2–3 days) is available for $25.
- Set up the customer's PIN selection (via phone IVR or at the branch terminal).
- Basic Checking: Visa Debit Card (daily ATM limit $500, purchase limit $5,000)
- Premium Checking: Visa Signature Debit Card (daily ATM limit $1,000, purchase limit $10,000)

## Step 7: Online Banking and Mobile App Setup

- Assist the customer in creating their online banking profile (username, password, security questions).
- Download and walk through the Solo Bank mobile app (if in-branch).
- Enable electronic statement delivery (optional; paper statements are free).
- Set up account alerts (low balance, large transactions, deposits).
- Demonstrate key features: mobile check deposit, bill pay, internal transfers, card lock/unlock.

## Step 8: Finalize and Document

- Review the completed account setup with the customer to confirm all details are correct.
- Print or email the account summary, including:
  - Account number and routing number
  - Product selected and key terms
  - Fee waiver requirements
  - Direct deposit form (for checking accounts)
  - Customer service contact information
- File all signed documents and disclosures in the customer's electronic file in the CBS.
- The CBS automatically generates a welcome letter mailed to the customer within 3 business days.

## Post-Opening Quality Check

Within 5 business days of account opening, the Branch Operations team performs a quality review:

- Verify all required documents are on file
- Confirm OFAC and ChexSystems results are documented
- Ensure all disclosures were provided and acknowledged
- Confirm the account was funded within policy requirements

## Online Account Opening Variations

For accounts opened through solobank.com:

- Identity is verified electronically using a combination of SSN verification, knowledge-based authentication questions, and device fingerprinting.
- OFAC and ChexSystems checks are performed automatically.
- The customer completes all disclosures and acknowledgments electronically.
- Funding is completed via ACH, wire, or debit card.
- If electronic identity verification fails, the customer is directed to visit a branch to complete the process in person.
