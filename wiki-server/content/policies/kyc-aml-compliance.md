# KYC/AML Compliance Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-KA-006
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026

## Overview

Solo Bank is committed to full compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), USA PATRIOT Act, and all FinCEN regulations. This policy outlines the Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) procedures that all employees must follow. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, regulatory penalties, and criminal liability.

## Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements

### Customer Identification Program (CIP)

All new customers must be verified through the following steps before any account is opened:

1. **Government-Issued Photo ID:** Collect and verify one of the following:
   - Valid U.S. driver's license or state ID
   - Valid U.S. passport or passport card
   - U.S. military ID
   - For non-U.S. persons: valid foreign passport with U.S. visa, or Permanent Resident Card

2. **Social Security Number (SSN) Verification:**
   - Collect the customer's SSN or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).
   - Verify the SSN/ITIN through the Social Security Administration database or an approved third-party vendor.
   - If the SSN cannot be verified electronically, require two additional forms of identification.

3. **Address Verification:**
   - Verify the customer's physical address (P.O. boxes alone are not acceptable as a primary address).
   - Acceptable verification: utility bill within 60 days, bank/brokerage statement, lease agreement, mortgage statement, or voter registration card.
   - Address must match across the ID and the verification document, or a satisfactory explanation must be documented.

4. **Date of Birth Verification:**
   - Must be verified against the government-issued ID.
   - Customer must be at least 18 years old to open an individual account (16 with a joint custodial account).

### Beneficial Ownership (Business Accounts)

For business accounts, Solo Bank must identify and verify the identity of:

- Any individual owning 25% or more of the entity.
- At least one individual with significant management responsibility (e.g., CEO, CFO, Managing Member).
- All beneficial owners must complete the standard CIP process.

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Procedures

### Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

- **Mandatory CTR filing** for any cash transaction of **$10,000 or more** (single transaction or aggregate in a business day).
- CTRs are filed with FinCEN within 15 calendar days of the transaction.
- Customers may not be informed that a CTR has been filed (tipping off is prohibited).

### Structuring Detection

Solo Bank monitors for structuring, defined as breaking up transactions to avoid the $10,000 reporting threshold:

- **Aggregate monitoring:** Any combination of cash deposits, withdrawals, or transfers totaling **$10,000 or more within a 24-hour period** across all of a customer's accounts triggers an automatic alert.
- **Pattern detection:** Multiple cash transactions between $8,000 and $9,999 within a 7-day period are flagged for review.
- **Multi-branch activity:** Cash transactions across different branches within a single day are aggregated and monitored.

### Wire Transfer Monitoring

All wire transfers are screened against:

- **OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List** — real-time screening on all outgoing and incoming wires.
- **High-Risk Jurisdictions:** Wire transfers involving the following require enhanced review and BSA Officer approval:
  - Countries on the FATF high-risk and non-cooperative jurisdictions list
  - Countries subject to U.S. sanctions programs
  - Known tax haven jurisdictions with no corresponding business relationship explanation
- **Threshold alerts:** Wire transfers of $3,000 or more require collection of originator and beneficiary information per the Travel Rule.

### Rapid Fund Movement

The following patterns trigger automatic alerts for rapid fund movement:

- Deposits followed by wire transfers or withdrawals of 80% or more of the deposited amount within 48 hours.
- Multiple incoming wires from different sources followed by a single outgoing wire within 72 hours.
- New accounts receiving deposits exceeding $25,000 within the first 30 days.

## Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

### Filing Requirements

- A SAR must be filed with FinCEN for any suspicious transaction or pattern of transactions of **$5,000 or more**.
- The SAR must be filed within **30 calendar days** of the initial detection of the suspicious activity.
- If no suspect is identified, the filing deadline extends to 60 calendar days.
- SARs are filed electronically through the FinCEN BSA E-Filing system.

### What Constitutes Suspicious Activity

- Transactions that have no apparent business or lawful purpose
- Transactions inconsistent with the customer's known profile or stated business
- Structuring or attempted structuring to evade reporting requirements
- Use of the account as a pass-through (funds in and out with no clear purpose)
- Customers who are unusually concerned about reporting requirements
- Customers who provide false or inconsistent identification documents

### SAR Confidentiality

- The existence of a SAR filing must **never** be disclosed to the customer or any unauthorized party.
- SAR-related information may only be shared with law enforcement upon receipt of a valid subpoena or National Security Letter.
- Internal access to SAR information is restricted to the BSA Officer, Compliance team, and senior management on a need-to-know basis.

## Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Enhanced Due Diligence is required for the following customer categories:

1. **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs):** Senior foreign political figures, their family members, and close associates.
2. **High-Risk Business Types:** Money service businesses, cryptocurrency exchanges, marijuana-related businesses (where state-legal), cash-intensive businesses, and non-profit organizations operating internationally.
3. **High-Value Accounts:** Any account with average monthly balances exceeding $1,000,000.
4. **Flagged Customers:** Any customer who has been the subject of a SAR filing or who has triggered 3 or more AML alerts within a 12-month period.

EDD measures include:
- More frequent account reviews (quarterly instead of annual)
- Source of funds and source of wealth documentation
- Senior management approval for account opening or continuation
- Ongoing monitoring with lower alert thresholds

## Employee Training

- All employees must complete BSA/AML training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.
- Customer-facing employees must complete additional KYC training specific to their role.
- The BSA Officer conducts quarterly updates on emerging AML risks and typologies.
- Training completion is tracked and audited annually.
