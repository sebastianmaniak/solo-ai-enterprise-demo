# Customer Service Escalation Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-ES-010
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** February 15, 2026

## Overview

Solo Bank's customer service escalation framework defines three levels of support authority, each with specific resolution powers, monetary limits, and service level agreements (SLAs). This policy ensures that customer issues are resolved efficiently at the appropriate level while maintaining consistent service quality.

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1 (L1) — Frontline Customer Service Representative

**Staffing:** Customer Service Representatives (phone, chat, email, and branch tellers)

**Authority:**

- **Fee Waiver:** May waive up to **$100** in fees per customer per calendar year. Individual fee waivers must not exceed $50 per incident.
- **Dispute Credit:** May issue provisional credits of up to **$500** for reported unauthorized transactions or billing errors.
- **Account Adjustments:** May correct posting errors up to $200, reverse one overdraft fee per customer per rolling 12 months as a goodwill gesture, and process address/contact information changes.
- **Product Support:** May assist with online banking password resets, debit card activations, statement requests, and general product inquiries.

**Cannot Do:**

- Cannot initiate fraud investigations (must escalate to L2)
- Cannot override credit decisions or modify interest rates
- Cannot waive fees exceeding their authority limits
- Cannot close accounts with outstanding balances or disputes
- Cannot process wire transfer exceptions

**SLA:** All L1 issues must be resolved or escalated within **24 hours** of initial contact.

**Escalation Trigger to L2:** Customer dissatisfaction with L1 resolution, monetary amounts exceeding L1 authority, suspected fraud, complex disputes, or customer request to speak with a supervisor.

### Level 2 (L2) — Customer Service Supervisor / Team Lead

**Staffing:** Customer Service Supervisors, Team Leads, and Branch Managers

**Authority:**

- **Fee Waiver:** May waive up to **$500** in fees per customer per calendar year.
- **Dispute Credit:** May issue provisional credits of up to **$5,000** for disputed transactions.
- **Fraud Investigation:** May initiate a formal fraud investigation and place temporary holds on suspected compromised accounts.
- **Rate Adjustments:** May authorize a one-time interest rate reduction of up to 1.00% on credit card accounts for retention purposes.
- **Account Modifications:** May override account restrictions, extend payment due dates (up to 30 days), and authorize exception processing for wire transfers.
- **Retention Offers:** May offer fee waivers, rate reductions, or bonus rewards points valued at up to $250 to retain a customer threatening to close accounts.

**Cannot Do:**

- Cannot waive fees exceeding $500 per customer per year
- Cannot authorize credits exceeding $5,000
- Cannot override compliance holds (AML/KYC/OFAC)
- Cannot approve exceptions to lending policy
- Cannot respond to regulatory complaints (must escalate to L3)

**SLA:** All L2 issues must be resolved or escalated within **4 hours** of receipt.

**Escalation Trigger to L3:** Regulatory complaints (OCC, CFPB, state AG), dispute amounts exceeding $5,000, media-related complaints, legal threats, unresolved customer issues after L2 intervention, or any complaint involving potential bank error resulting in material customer harm.

### Level 3 (L3) — Senior Management / Executive Resolution

**Staffing:** VP of Customer Experience, Chief Operations Officer, Chief Compliance Officer, and designated Executive Resolution Specialists

**Authority:**

- **Fee Waiver:** **Unlimited** fee waiver authority.
- **Dispute Credit:** **Unlimited** provisional credit authority (subject to documentation and audit review).
- **Regulatory Response:** Authorized to respond to formal complaints from regulatory agencies (OCC, CFPB, FDIC, state banking regulators).
- **Policy Exceptions:** May authorize exceptions to any bank policy with documented justification.
- **Legal Coordination:** May coordinate with Legal department on threatened or actual litigation.
- **Remediation Programs:** May design and authorize customer remediation programs for systemic issues.
- **Public Relations:** May coordinate with Marketing/PR on complaints that pose reputational risk.

**SLA:** All L3 issues must have initial acknowledgment within **1 hour** of receipt and a resolution plan within **24 hours**. Full resolution target is 5 business days, with weekly status updates to the customer for issues requiring extended investigation.

## Escalation Process

### How to Escalate

1. **L1 to L2:** L1 representative documents the issue, actions taken, and reason for escalation in the CRM case notes. The case is reassigned to the L2 queue. If the customer is on the phone, a warm transfer is performed to an available L2 supervisor.

2. **L2 to L3:** L2 supervisor documents the issue, all actions taken at both levels, and the specific reason for escalation. An email notification is sent to the L3 on-call executive. For urgent matters (regulatory, legal, media), the L2 supervisor also calls the L3 on-call number directly.

3. **Direct to L3:** Certain issue types bypass L1/L2 and go directly to L3:
   - Formal regulatory complaints (CFPB, OCC, etc.)
   - Complaints from customers who are attorneys threatening legal action
   - Media inquiries about customer complaints
   - Complaints involving potential violations of law
   - Any complaint from a customer with total relationship balances exceeding $1,000,000

### Customer Communication During Escalation

- The customer must be informed that their issue is being escalated to a higher level.
- The customer must be given a case reference number and an estimated time for follow-up.
- The customer should not have to re-explain their issue at each level — case notes must be thorough.
- If the SLA for any level is at risk of being breached, the assigned representative must proactively contact the customer with a status update.

## Documentation Requirements

All escalated interactions must include:

1. Customer name, account number, and contact information
2. Date and time of initial contact and each subsequent interaction
3. Detailed description of the customer's complaint or issue
4. Actions taken at each escalation level
5. Resolution provided (including monetary value of any credits, waivers, or adjustments)
6. Customer's stated satisfaction with the resolution
7. Root cause analysis (for L2 and L3 cases)

## Reporting and Quality Assurance

- **Weekly Report:** L2 and L3 case volumes, resolution times, and SLA compliance are reported to the VP of Customer Experience weekly.
- **Monthly Review:** Trends in escalation reasons are analyzed monthly. Systemic issues identified through escalation patterns are reported to the relevant department head for corrective action.
- **Quality Monitoring:** 10% of L1 calls and 25% of L2 calls are reviewed for quality and policy compliance monthly.
- **Customer Satisfaction:** Post-resolution surveys are sent for all L2 and L3 cases. Target CSAT score: 85% or higher.
