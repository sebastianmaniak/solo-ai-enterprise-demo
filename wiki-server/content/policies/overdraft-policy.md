# Overdraft Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-OD-005
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** February 1, 2026

## Overview

Solo Bank offers three forms of overdraft coverage to help customers manage situations where their account balance is insufficient to cover a transaction. Customers must affirmatively opt in to overdraft services for ATM and one-time debit card transactions, as required by Regulation E.

## Regulation E Compliance

Under Regulation E, Solo Bank **cannot** charge overdraft fees on ATM withdrawals or one-time debit card transactions unless the customer has opted in to the Standard Overdraft Service. This opt-in requirement does not apply to checks, ACH debits, or recurring debit card transactions, which may be covered under the Standard Overdraft Service regardless of opt-in status.

- Customers may opt in or opt out at any time through online banking, by phone, or at a branch.
- Opt-in status is displayed on the customer's account settings page.
- Solo Bank sends a confirmation notice within 3 business days of any opt-in or opt-out change.

## Standard Overdraft Service

The Standard Overdraft Service allows transactions to be paid when the checking account has insufficient funds, at the bank's discretion.

- **Overdraft Fee:** $35 per transaction
- **Maximum Overdraft Fees Per Day:** 3 (maximum daily overdraft charges = $105)
- **De Minimis Threshold:** No overdraft fee is charged if the account is overdrawn by $5.00 or less at the end of the business day.
- **Grace Period:** If the account is brought to a positive balance by 11:59 PM ET on the next business day, the overdraft fee is waived.
- **Coverage Limit:** Solo Bank may, at its discretion, cover overdrafts up to $500 for Basic Checking and up to $1,000 for Premium Checking. This is not a guaranteed line of credit.
- **Continuous Overdraft Fee:** If the account remains overdrawn for 5 consecutive business days, an additional $7.00 per day continuous overdraft fee applies, up to a maximum of $35 (5 days).
- **Account Closure Risk:** Accounts overdrawn for 30 or more consecutive calendar days may be closed and the balance reported to ChexSystems.

### Transaction Posting Order

Transactions are posted in the following order, which affects when overdraft fees are assessed:

1. Credits (deposits, incoming transfers)
2. Debits in chronological order of authorization

## Overdraft Protection (Linked Savings Transfer)

Customers may link a Solo Bank savings account to their checking account for Overdraft Protection. When the checking account has insufficient funds, the bank automatically transfers funds from the linked savings account.

- **Transfer Fee:** $12 per transfer
- **Transfer Increments:** Funds are transferred in $100 increments, covering the exact shortfall rounded up to the nearest $100.
- **Maximum Transfers Per Day:** 6 transfers
- **Savings Account Minimum:** The linked savings account must maintain a minimum balance of $25 after the transfer. If the savings balance is insufficient, the transfer will not occur and the transaction may be processed under the Standard Overdraft Service (if opted in) or declined.
- **Eligible Accounts:** Basic Savings, High-Yield Savings, and Money Market accounts may be linked.
- **Setup:** Customers can set up Overdraft Protection through online banking, by phone, or at a branch.

## Overdraft Line of Credit

Qualifying customers may apply for an Overdraft Line of Credit, which functions as a revolving credit line attached to the checking account.

- **Interest Rate:** Variable, currently Prime Rate + 8.00% (as of April 1, 2026, this equals 16.50% APR). No per-transaction fee.
- **Minimum Line Amount:** $500
- **Maximum Line Amount:** Determined by credit tier:
  - Tier 1: Up to $10,000
  - Tier 2: Up to $7,500
  - Tier 3: Up to $5,000
  - Tier 4: Up to $2,500
  - Tier 5: Not eligible
- **Minimum Payment:** Greater of $25 or 2% of the outstanding balance, due monthly.
- **Eligibility:** Requires a credit application and approval. Minimum credit score of 580 (Tier 4 or above). The account must be open for at least 6 months.
- **Advances:** Automatic — when the checking account goes negative, the line of credit is drawn in the exact amount needed to cover the shortfall. No manual action required.
- **Interest Calculation:** Interest accrues daily on the outstanding balance from the date of the advance until repaid.

## Opting Out

Customers who do not wish to have any overdraft coverage may opt out entirely. When opted out:

- ATM and one-time debit card transactions will be declined if the account has insufficient funds.
- Checks and ACH debits may still be returned unpaid, resulting in a **Returned Item Fee of $35**.
- Recurring debit card payments may be declined at the merchant.

## Fee Comparison Summary

| Feature | Standard Overdraft | Overdraft Protection | Overdraft Line of Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost | $35 per item | $12 per transfer | Variable APR (no per-item fee) |
| Daily Max Charges | 3 ($105) | 6 transfers ($72) | No cap (interest only) |
| Opt-In Required (Reg E) | Yes | No (auto-transfers) | No (credit product) |
| Credit Check Required | No | No | Yes |
| Covers Checks/ACH | Yes (auto) | Yes (auto) | Yes (auto) |
| Covers ATM/Debit | Only if opted in | Yes | Yes |

## Customer Communication

- Overdraft notices are sent via email and/or push notification within 1 business day of the overdraft event.
- Monthly statements include a summary of all overdraft fees charged during the statement period.
- An annual Overdraft Usage Summary is mailed to customers who incurred more than $100 in overdraft fees during the calendar year, along with information about alternative overdraft options.
